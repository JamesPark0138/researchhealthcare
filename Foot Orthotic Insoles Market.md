<p data-start="731" data-end="1291"><strong data-start="731" data-end="753">Market Definition:</strong> <strong data-start="754" data-end="779">Foot orthotic insoles</strong> are specially designed inserts that are placed inside shoes to correct or enhance the function of the foot. These insoles are used to provide support, cushioning, and alignment for the foot, helping to alleviate pain caused by foot conditions such as flat feet, plantar fasciitis, arthritis, and heel spurs. Foot orthotics can be custom-made or prefabricated and are often used in the treatment of foot-related disorders, as well as for general comfort, especially for people who stand or walk for long periods.</p>
