<p data-start="728" data-end="750"><strong data-start="728" data-end="750">Market Definition:</strong></p>
<p data-start="752" data-end="1369">A surgical stapler is a medical device used to close wounds or surgical incisions by using staples to hold tissues together. It is typically employed during surgeries to provide a quicker and more efficient method of wound closure, reducing the need for sutures and promoting faster recovery. Surgical staplers offer advantages over traditional sutures, including enhanced precision, fewer complications, and reduced operation times. The <strong data-start="1190" data-end="1217">surgical stapler market</strong> is driven by advancements in stapler designs, including the introduction of robotic staplers, and the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures.</p>
