<p data-start="773" data-end="1475"><strong data-start="773" data-end="795">Market Definition:</strong><strong data-start="796" data-end="823">Bone growth stimulators</strong> are medical devices designed to aid in the healing of bone fractures, particularly in cases where fractures do not heal properly (non-union) or when there is a need to accelerate the healing process. These stimulators work by using electrical, electromagnetic, or ultrasonic fields to stimulate bone growth and accelerate the healing of fractures. They are commonly used in orthopedic treatments, including spinal fusion surgeries, fractures, and degenerative bone conditions. Bone growth stimulators can be used both in clinical settings and at home, and they are particularly beneficial for patients who suffer from delayed or non-healing fractures.</p>
